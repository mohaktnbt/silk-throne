*set chapter 3

Sleep does not come. You lie in the dead man's bed and stare at the painted ceiling — heroes and gods locked in eternal, meaningless combat — and you turn the pieces in your mind.

Your predecessor, Grand Vizier Osman, is dead. Murdered, if the hooded woman is to be believed. And someone in this palace — someone who dines with the Emperor, who walks these marble corridors, who smiles at you across council tables — is responsible.

By dawn, you have made a decision. You will not wait for the truth to find you. You will hunt it.

*page_break

Farid arrives with tea and finds you already dressed.

"You did not sleep," he says. It is not a question.

"I need access to Osman's study," you tell him. "The real study — not the official one. A man like Osman would have had a private workspace."

Farid hesitates. Then, slowly, he nods. "There is a room. Behind the library, through a passage that is not on any palace map. Osman showed it to me once, when he needed a letter delivered without the usual channels." He pauses. "I have not been back since his death. I was afraid of what I might find."

"Show me."

*page_break

The passage is narrow and unlit — a servant's corridor from an older era of the palace, before the current dynasty expanded the eastern wing. Farid leads you through it with a small oil lamp, and the walls press close enough that your shoulders brush the stone. The air smells of dust and old plaster and something faintly chemical — ink, you realize. Old ink.

The hidden study is small. A desk. A chair. A shelf of books. No windows. A single lamp bracket on the wall.

And it has been searched.

The desk drawers hang open, emptied. The books have been pulled from the shelf and restacked carelessly. Someone was here before you, looking for the same thing you are looking for. But they were in a hurry, and people in a hurry make mistakes.

*page_break

You search methodically. Farid watches the door.

You recognize two: a provincial governor and a merchant you have never heard of. The other four are unknown to you.

    At the bottom, in different ink, Osman wrote a single word: "Witnesses."

*page_break

Farid examines what you have found. His face is grey in the lamplight.

"Grand Vizier, whatever Osman discovered, it was dangerous enough to get him killed. And whoever killed him is still looking for the evidence he gathered." He meets your eyes. "They will come looking for you next, once they learn you have been here."

From somewhere above, a bell begins to toll — the morning audience bell. The court will be assembling. Your absence will be noted.

You pocket the evidence and follow Farid back through the hidden passage, the weight of Osman's secrets pressing against your chest like a second heartbeat.

The investigation has only just begun. But already, one thing is clear: the murder of Grand Vizier Osman was not the act of a lone assassin. It was a conspiracy. And the threads of that conspiracy lead to the highest levels of the Khazaran court.

The question is no longer who killed Osman.

The question is who you can trust.
