*comment === OPENING: THE LAST NIGHT ===

The sky over Khazara is the color of a bruise.

Purple and gold, streaked with the last violent light of a sun that descends behind the Ashghar Mountains as if it knows what tomorrow brings and does not wish to watch. The western peaks are silhouetted black against the bleeding sky, their snow-capped ridges catching the final rays and throwing them back as tiny fires -- the mountain passes where the empire's trade flows, where the caravans carry silk and spice and the wealth of nations, where the conspiracy fed secrets to the Khanates and watched the empire bleed.

The city is quiet tonight -- quieter than you have ever heard it. The Grand Bazaar closed early, its vaulted corridors dark and hollow, the merchants gone home to their families and their ledgers and the ordinary evening rituals that make a life. The garrison gates are shut, the soldiers drilling in the fading light with an urgency that speaks of rumors filtering down through the ranks. Even the fountains in the Garden of Eternal Spring sound different tonight, their music carrying a minor key, a note of farewell, as if the water knows something the stone does not.

You stand on the balcony of your chambers and breathe.

The air smells of jasmine and woodsmoke and the clean mineral scent of the Tavan River, the great artery that has fed this city for three centuries. Somewhere in the Temple Quarter, a single voice chants the evening prayer, the melody rising and falling with the practiced grace of a devotion performed ten thousand times. The sound threads through the purple dusk like a silver needle through dark silk, stitching together the disparate pieces of the city -- the rich and the poor, the powerful and the forgotten, the faithful and the doubting -- into a single fabric that might, with care, hold.

Tomorrow, you move against the conspiracy.

Tomorrow, everything changes.

But tonight -- tonight is yours. These last hours before the storm, when the air is heavy with possibility and the city holds its breath. You have preparations to make. Alliances to secure. People to see.

People who matter.

You breathe the evening air. It carries the smells of the city -- the jasmine from the garden below, the woodsmoke from the kitchens where tomorrow's bread is already baking, the faint mineral tang of the Tavan River, the incense from the Temple Quarter.
