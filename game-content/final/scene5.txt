*comment === OPENING: MORNING IN KHAZARA ===

Dawn comes to Khazara the way a lover enters a room -- slowly, then all at once.

You stand on your balcony and watch the city wake. First the call to prayer, rising from the Temple Quarter like smoke made into sound, threading between the minarets and domes until it fills the sky. Then the light -- pale gold pouring over the eastern rooftops, catching the turquoise tilework of the Grand Bazaar, gilding the slow brown waters of the Ashar where it bends past the River District.

Below you, the Garden of Eternal Spring stirs with the industry of early morning: gardeners pruning the rose arbors, servants carrying trays of tea and fresh bread along the colonnaded paths, two peacocks strutting past the central fountain as though they own the place. The smell of jasmine rises on warm air, mingling with cardamom from the palace kitchens.

It is the first morning since your arrival that you do not wake to a crisis.

The thought settles over you with an unfamiliar lightness. No anonymous threats slipped under your door. No emergency summons from the Council. No dead men's offices to search, no poison vials to examine, no spymasters materializing from shadows to deliver revelations that rearrange the architecture of your understanding. Just a morning. Just a city. Just the smell of jasmine and the sound of water and the weight of the seal ring on your finger, which today feels less like a burden and more like a key.

*page_break

Farid appears at your door, predictably early, carrying a leather folio and a cup of cardamom tea. He has the look of a man who has been awake for hours and is slightly offended that the sun took so long to join him.

"My ${title_honorific} Vizier. Good morning. Today's schedule is -- well." He pauses. Consults the folio. Consults it again. "Today's schedule is remarkably open. You have no Council meetings. No formal audiences. No crises that I am aware of, though the day is young."

He says this last part with the air of a man who has learned that Khazara generates crises the way the Ashar generates mosquitoes.

"Guildmaster Priya's invitation stands for the Bazaar. Beyond that, several opportunities have presented themselves. Captain Navid has extended an invitation to observe the garrison drills. You take the tea. It is perfect -- strong, sweet, fragrant with cardamom. You look out at the city spread below you like a tapestry, two million souls stirring in the morning light.

Three choices. Three windows into the heart of an empire.
