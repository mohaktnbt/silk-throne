*comment === THE FESTIVAL BEGINS ===

*label festival_opening

The city is on fire.

Not the fire of destruction -- the fire of ten thousand oil lamps, each one placed by hand along every street, every bridge, every rooftop in Khazara. The Festival of Lights has transformed the capital into something that belongs more to dream than to waking life. From the heights of the Citadel, the city below looks like a fallen constellation, its reflected glow turning the Ashar and Tavan rivers into ribbons of liquid gold.

You stand on the balcony of the Grand Terrace, dressed in festival robes of deep crimson silk embroidered with thread-of-gold. The Vizier's seal ring catches the lamplight on your finger. The air smells of jasmine and rosewater and the sweet smoke of sandalwood incense rising from a hundred braziers.

Below, the Citadel's Garden of Eternal Spring has been transformed. Silk canopies in saffron and midnight blue stretch between the cypress trees. Musicians play from a raised pavilion -- the liquid notes of the tar weaving through the steady heartbeat of the daf, the ney's voice rising above it all like a prayer given sound.

*page_break

You have read about the Festival of Lights in the archives -- the histories, the poems, the administrative records that meticulously catalogue each year's celebration down to the number of lamps lit and the cost per barrel of oil. But the records did not prepare you for the reality. No amount of paper captures what it feels like to stand at the edge of a city transformed by ten thousand points of flame, each one a small defiance against the dark.

Two million people celebrate tonight. The Festival of Lights falls on the autumn equinox, the night when darkness and light stand in perfect balance. By tradition, the lamps are lit at sunset and burn until dawn, holding back the dark. The Eternal Flame in the Temple of Seven Wisdoms burns brighter than any other night of the year, visible from every quarter of the city.

It is the empire's most sacred holiday. It is also, as Farid has reminded you three times today, the most politically significant night of the year.

"My lord Vizier," Farid says at your elbow, clutching a sheaf of parchment. "The -- the guest list. I have confirmed two hundred and forty-seven attendees of note. Seventeen ambassadors. Nine provincial governors. The full Council of Five. And -- and the imperial family, of course. Including --" He pauses. "Prince Kaveh has confirmed his attendance."

The name lands like a stone in still water.

*comment === THE EMPEROR'S ENTRANCE ===

*label emperor_entrance

A trumpet sounds -- three rising notes, ancient and commanding. The crowd stills. The music fades. Every head turns toward the Garden's grand staircase, where two lines of imperial guards in ceremonial armor stand at attention, their spear-tips catching the lamplight like stars.

Emperor Roshan IV descends.

He is dressed in white -- the pure white that only the Emperor may wear -- with a cloak of cloth-of-gold flowing behind him. The Crown of the Seven Wisdoms, a circlet of white jade set with seven stones, sits upon his brow. At sixteen, he is still growing into his own frame, still learning how to carry the weight of the crown without tilting his head. But tonight -- tonight he looks like an emperor.

The Dowager Empress Nur descends three steps behind, as protocol demands. She wears deepest purple, her hair threaded with gold chains, her expression as composed as a painted mask. Her eyes sweep the crowd with the practiced assessment of a woman who has attended these festivals for thirty years.

*page_break

The crowd bows. Two hundred and forty-seven people of consequence, seventeen ambassadors, nine provincial governors, and one Grand Vizier lower their heads in unison. The rustle of silk is like a wind through leaves. The clink of jewelry and ceremonial weapons creates a brief, tinkling music beneath the formal silence.

You bow. The seal ring touches the fabric of your robes. The Empire's ritual machinery engages its gears, and for this moment -- this single, suspended moment -- the dynasty's three-hundred-year claim to divine authority is renewed in fire and prostration and the ancient, unbroken compact between ruler and ruled.

"Light of the World," Ashoka intones, stepping forward with the ceremonial lamp. "Shadow of God upon the Earth. The Flame welcomes its guardian."

The Emperor takes the lamp. His hands are steady -- you note this with something that might be pride. He carries it to the great brazier at the Garden's center and touches the wick to the sacred oil.

The flame leaps. The crowd gasps, then cheers. The Festival is officially begun.
