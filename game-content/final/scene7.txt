The Citadel is a wound torn open in the dark.

You run through corridors you have walked a hundred times, but tonight they are a place you do not recognize. Torches gutter in iron sconces, throwing shadows that leap and twist against the marble walls. Guards sprint past you in pairs, swords drawn, their faces white with the particular terror of soldiers who have failed their single duty.

The smell of smoke is stronger here. Not the warm smoke of hearth and incense but something sharper, chemical -- a fire that was set, not kindled.

Farid keeps pace beside you, his robes bunched in one fist to keep from tripping. He has not stopped talking since you left your chambers, a breathless litany of fragments: "The royal apartments -- a guard was killed -- Captain Navid sounded the alarm -- the Dowager is -- the Emperor is alive, they say he's alive--"

"They say?" The words come out harder than you intend.

"He's alive, my ${title_honorific} Vizier. He's alive. Captain Navid is with him."

*page_break

You round the corner into the imperial wing and the world narrows to a single point.

Blood on white marble. A dark sprawl of it, still wet, still catching the torchlight in a way that makes your stomach clench. A shape on the floor -- a body, covered now with a guard's cloak, but the hand that protrudes from beneath the fabric is still curled around a sword hilt. The dead guard. Corporal Behzad, Farid whispers. Twenty-three years old. He was supposed to be married next month.

Beyond the body, the doors to the Emperor's private chambers stand open. One has been wrenched half off its hinges. The wood is splintered at shoulder height, as if something -- someone -- was thrown through it.

Captain Navid stands in the doorway. His sword is drawn and there is blood on the blade, though you cannot tell whose. His face is a mask of controlled fury -- the expression of a soldier who arrived thirty seconds too late and will never forgive himself for it.

"Vizier," he says. His voice is iron wrapped in ice. "The Emperor is inside. He is unharmed. The assassin has fled."

"Fled where?"

"If I knew that, the assassin would be dead and I would not be standing here." He steps aside to let you pass. "Be careful. We have not cleared all the rooms."

*page_break

The Emperor's chambers have been violated.

The receiving room is in disarray -- a table overturned, a shattered vase leaking water across the floor, cushions slashed open so that feathers drift through the air like strange snow. The smell of smoke comes from a small fire that burns in the corner where an oil lamp was knocked over and caught a curtain. Two guards are beating it out with cloaks.

And in the center of the room, sitting on the edge of his sleeping platform with a blanket wrapped around his shoulders, is Emperor Roshan IV, Light of the World, Shadow of God upon the Earth.

He is sixteen years old. In this moment, he looks twelve.

His eyes are wide, dark, and dry. He has not cried. You suspect he is beyond crying -- in that hollow place past fear where the body simply waits for the next thing to happen. There is a cut on his left hand, shallow but bleeding, where he must have caught himself on broken glass. No one has tended to it.

He sees you. Something in his face shifts -- a tiny loosening, as if a string drawn too tight has slackened by a single degree.

"Vizier," he says. His voice is steady. He is trying so hard for it to be steady. "Someone tried to kill me."
