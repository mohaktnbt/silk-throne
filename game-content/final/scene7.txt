*comment === OPENING: CHAOS IN THE CITADEL ===

The Citadel is a wound torn open in the dark.

You run through corridors you have walked a hundred times in daylight, but tonight they are a place you do not recognize. The marble floors that gleam like milk under the afternoon sun are slick with shadow. Torches gutter in iron sconces, throwing shapes against the walls that leap and twist and reach -- shapes that look, in the animal part of your brain, like grasping hands.

Guards sprint past you in pairs, swords drawn, their boots hammering the stone in urgent percussion. Their faces are white. These are soldiers trained to fight, trained to hold the line against Khanate raiders and frontier bandits, and they are afraid. You can smell it on them -- the sharp tang of sweat that comes from fear, not exertion. They have failed their single, sacred duty. The Emperor was attacked in his own chambers, behind his own walls, under their watch.

The smell of smoke is stronger as you approach the imperial wing. Not the warm smoke of hearth and incense that threads through every room of the Citadel like a fifth element. This is sharper, chemical -- a fire that was set, not kindled. Something is burning that was never meant to burn.

Farid keeps pace beside you, his robes bunched in one fist to keep from tripping, his sandals slapping the marble in a frantic rhythm that counterpoints the heavier beat of your own footsteps. He has not stopped talking since you left your chambers, his voice a breathless litany of fragments assembled from rumor and terror:

"The royal apartments -- a guard was killed -- Captain Navid sounded the alarm -- the Dowager is -- the Emperor is alive, they say he's alive--"

"They say?" The words come out harder than you intend.

Farid flinches. "He's alive, my ${title_honorific} Vizier. He's alive. Captain Navid is with him. Navid confirmed it himself, by runner. The Emperor is alive."

Something in your chest loosens -- a tension you did not know you were carrying, wound as tight as the mechanism of a Citadel clock.

You round the corner into the imperial wing and the world narrows to a single point.

Blood on white marble.

A dark sprawl of it, already thickening at the edges, still wet at the center where it catches the torchlight and reflects it back in deep, terrible crimson. The blood has pooled in the shallow channel between two tiles, filling the geometric pattern with a color the architects never intended. It is the most obscene thing you have ever seen -- not because of the blood itself, but because of the beauty it violates. This is a floor designed for the footsteps of emperors. No one planned for it to hold this.

A shape lies on the floor. A body, covered now with a guard's cloak, the fabric already darkening with what soaks through from beneath. But the hand that protrudes from under the edge of the cloak is still curled around a sword hilt. Still holding the weapon, even in death. He sees you. Something in his face shifts -- a tiny loosening, as if a string drawn too tight has slackened by a single degree. Not relief, exactly. Something more fragile than relief. Recognition, perhaps. The knowledge that someone he trusts has arrived.

"Vizier," he says. His voice is steady. He is trying very hard for it to be steady. He is trying the way his ancestor Roshan the First must have tried, three hundred years ago, to sound like a ruler when the world was on fire. "Someone tried to kill me."
