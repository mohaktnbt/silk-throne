The Grand Festival of Lights is three days away.

You can feel the city gathering itself, the way a river gathers speed before a waterfall. Merchants drape their stalls in crimson and gold. Temple acolytes polish the great bronze brazier of the Eternal Flame until it gleams like a second sun. Children run through the streets trailing paper lanterns, their laughter bright in the cooling autumn air.

In the Citadel, the preparations are grander and quieter. Servants hang silk banners in every corridor. The kitchens have been working since before dawn, and the smell of saffron and roasting almonds drifts through the marble halls like a benevolent ghost. The Garden of Eternal Spring is being transformed into a stage for the Emperor's address, with terraced seating, a speaker's platform, and a thousand oil lamps that will be lit at sunset on the festival night.

*page_break

Farid finds you after the morning briefing, his arms full of documents and his face full of the particular anxiety that precedes large-scale events.

"My lord Vizier, the festival preparations require your attention on -- let me see -- fourteen separate matters, ranging from the Emperor's seating arrangement to the allocation of lamp oil, which has become surprisingly contentious. The Temple Quarter is requesting double their usual allotment and General Kadir is insisting that the garrison receive enough to light the watchtowers, which is reasonable but leaves the River District short by approximately forty barrels, and --"

He catches your expression and stops.

"But perhaps," he says, more quietly, "you have other plans for the day."

You do. The festival is important, but it is also cover. Three days of celebration mean three days of loosened security, distracted guards, and crowded streets where conspirators can move unseen. You need to use the time before the festival to prepare -- to strengthen alliances, gather intelligence, and shore up the defenses that matter most.

You have time for three tasks before the festival preparations consume the remainder of the day.
