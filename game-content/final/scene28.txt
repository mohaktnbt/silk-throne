*comment === OPENING: DAWN ===

Dawn comes to Khazara the color of blood.

The eastern sky splits along the horizon, red and gold and violent orange, as though the sun itself is wounded. The light spills across the city's rooftops in a slow, inexorable tide, catching the domes and minarets, painting the Ashar River in shades of fire and copper. The mist that hangs over the water burns off in ragged wisps, revealing the bridges one by one -- seventeen bridges, the oldest lined with shuttered shops, all of them silent this morning.

From the Citadel walls, you can see the entire breadth of the capital -- two million souls waking to the day that will determine whether their empire endures. The Grand Bazaar is closed for the first time in living memory. The Temple Quarter is quiet except for the priests who tend the Eternal Flame. The streets, usually bustling with merchants and laborers and children running errands, are empty. The city holds its breath.

In the distance, beyond the city's southern gates, you can see them.

Kaveh's army.

*page_break

Three thousand strong, arrayed in loose formation on the plain between the city and the river's southern bend. From this distance they look almost abstract -- a smear of color against the brown earth, a geometry of formations that could be mistaken for a festival gathering if not for the glint of steel.

Their banners are crimson and gold -- the colors of Prince Kaveh's branch of the royal family, colors that by law should be displayed only in the Citadel, only with the Emperor's permission. The presumption of it burns like acid in your throat. Kaveh has not merely challenged the throne. He has dressed his rebellion in the throne's own colors.

Through the Tower of Stars' finest telescope, brought to the walls at your order and mounted on a wooden tripod by Scholar Miriam's trembling hands, you can make out the details. Cavalry on the flanks -- noble house guards on trained warhorses, their lances catching the dawn light like needles. Infantry in the center -- a mix of disaffected garrison soldiers and hired mercenaries, armored in everything from military-issue steel to boiled leather and chainmail. Siege equipment at the rear: scaling ladders, a battering ram tipped with iron, and two small cannons that must have been smuggled from a provincial armory, their bronze barrels gleaming with fresh oil.

Kaveh's army is a patchwork. But it is armed, it is organized, and it is moving toward the city with the deliberate pace of an approaching storm.

And to the north, too far to see but never far from your thoughts, the Khanate horde presses against the Wall of Roshan. Twelve thousand horse warriors, their compound bows strung, their scimitars sharpened, waiting for the signal that the empire's capital has fallen so they can pour through the gaps. General Kadir's dispatches arrived an hour ago, written in the terse, clipped hand of a man who has no time for penmanship: *The Wall holds. Garrison committed. Cannot reinforce capital. Make your fight fast.*

*page_break

Fast. As though the fate of three centuries can be decided on a schedule. As though an empire's survival can be measured in hours and minutes, like a merchant counting the moments until a deal closes.

You descend from the wall to the Citadel courtyard, where your forces are assembled. The imperial guard -- Navid's command -- stands in precise formation, armor gleaming, spears vertical. The temple guards, if they are with you, occupy the eastern courtyard, their white robes a stark contrast to the soldiers' steel.

*if (romance_navid)

Farid appears at your elbow. He is wearing, for the first time in his life, a leather breastplate over his secretary's robes. It does not fit well. He looks like a tortoise that has grown a second shell.

"My lord Vizier. All units are in position. The city gates are sealed per regulation-- per common sense. The evacuation routes are prepared. The Council has been notified." He swallows. "I have also taken the liberty of writing my will. Just -- just in case. It is filed under 'F' for 'Farid' in the Vizier's office archive."

"You're not going to die, Farid."

"Statistically, my lord Vizier, the mortality rate in imperial sieges is--"

"Farid."

"Yes, my lord Vizier. Not dying. Understood."

*comment === MILITARY STRATEGY OPENING ===
*label military_opening

Your plan is direct: meet Kaveh's army at the Ashar Bridge, the narrow stone crossing that controls the only approach to the capital from the south. The bridge is wide enough for ten soldiers abreast -- a chokepoint that negates Kaveh's numerical advantage.

*if (final_alliance_military)
  Kadir's best captain -- a woman named Tahmineh, battle-scarred and iron-voiced -- commands the bridge defense with two thousand of the empire's finest soldiers. They have had the night to prepare: barricades at the bridge's midpoint, archers on the flanks, oil reserves for the fire traps Kadir designed.
*else
  Navid's imperial guard holds the bridge -- a thousand soldiers, superbly trained but outnumbered three to one. The barricades are hastily constructed. The flanking positions are thin. It will have to be enough.

You stand behind the front line, close enough to see the enemy's faces when they come. The Ashar River churns beneath the ancient stone, brown and fast with the season's rains. The bridge trembles faintly under the weight of armed soldiers.

Kaveh's forces approach in the rising light. You see the prince himself -- mounted on a white horse at the center of his line, golden armor catching the sun. Even at this distance, you can see the smile. He expects to win.

*page_break

The first assault hits the bridge like a wave striking a seawall.

Kaveh's mercenaries charge with a roar, swords and spears raised, shields locked. The bridge narrows them, funnels them into the barricade, and the defenders hold. Steel rings on steel. Men scream. The air fills with the stink of blood and sweat and river water.

The bridge holds.

The second assault comes twenty minutes later -- harder, more organized, with ladders lowered to the river banks to try flanking positions. Archers on the south bank trade volleys with your own. A bolt whistles past your ear, close enough to stir your hair.

The bridge holds. But the defenders are bleeding. You see gaps in the line where soldiers have fallen, filled by others stepping forward with grim determination.
