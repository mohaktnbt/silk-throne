*comment === OPENING: MORNING AFTER ===

You wake to the smell of jasmine and smoke.

For a moment -- one merciful moment -- you do not remember. The morning light falls across your sleeping platform in warm bars of gold, and the garden fountains murmur their eternal conversation outside the window, and you are simply a person waking in a beautiful room in the greatest city in the world.

Then memory returns, and the weight of it presses you back against the pillows.

The Emperor. The blood on white marble. The calling card with its familiar, terrible symbol. The longest night, and the morning that followed it. The three paths that crystallized in your mind like frost on glass.

You rise. Your body aches -- you slept three hours, perhaps four, after the crisis was contained. The seal ring sits on the bedside table where you placed it before collapsing into bed. You pick it up. It is warm from the sunlight, and it fits your finger now in a way it did not when you first arrived in Khazara. You have grown into it. Or it has shaped itself to you.

*page_break

Farid arrives with breakfast: flatbread, soft cheese, honey, figs, and a pot of cardamom tea that smells like every good morning you have ever had. He sets the tray down with his usual careful precision, then stands at attention with his hands clasped behind his back.

He looks as though he has not slept at all.

"The situation, my ${title_honorific} Vizier," he begins, and you hear the faintest tremor in his voice. "General Kadir has tripled the Citadel guard. The Dowager Empress has convened a private meeting of the noble houses for this afternoon. High Priest Ashoka is conducting prayers for the Emperor's safety at the Temple of Seven Wisdoms -- the public has been told there was a 'disturbance,' not a full assassination attempt, but rumors are spreading."

He pauses for breath.

"Spymaster Zara has not been seen since before dawn. Her agents are moving through the Old City but will not say why. Guildmaster Priya sent a message asking whether the trade embargo negotiations should continue or be suspended. Captain Navid has requested permission to conduct a sweep of the palace sewer system, which I did not realize we had until he mentioned it."

"And the Emperor?"

Farid's expression softens. "Alive. Guarded. The Dowager is with him. He asked to see you this afternoon."

*page_break

You eat. The bread is warm, the cheese sharp, the figs sweet enough to make your teeth ache. Through the window, Khazara stretches to the horizon -- domes and minarets catching the sun, the two rivers glittering like hammered silver, the Grand Bazaar already humming with commerce despite the night's violence. The city does not stop. It never stops. Two million souls going about their business, trusting that the people in this Citadel will keep them safe.

The thought is staggering. And clarifying.

You pour cardamom tea and let the steam rise between your hands. The fragrance fills the room -- warm, complex, grounding. You think about what you know. What you have learned in these weeks since a nervous secretary met you at the city gates and placed a seal ring on your finger and said, in his flustered, earnest way, that the empire needed you.

The conspiracy is real. It reaches into the Citadel itself -- into the guard rotations, the intelligence networks, the treasury. Someone with power and resources is systematically weakening the empire from within: inciting the Khanate raids, provoking the trade embargo, and now attempting to kill the Emperor himself.

The factions are fracturing. Each one sees the crisis through the lens of their own interests. Kadir sees a military failure requiring military solutions. Priya sees an economic threat requiring economic answers. Ashoka sees a moral crisis. Zara sees intelligence failures. The Dowager sees her son in danger and reaches for the only tool she knows: control.

And the Emperor. The boy on the throne. Sixteen years old, frightened, brave, more intelligent than anyone gives him credit for. Asking: *What should I do?*

You stand. You walk to the window one last time. The sun is directly overhead now, and the city is blazing. Every dome and minaret is a torch of reflected light. The Eternal Flame in the Temple of Seven Wisdoms is invisible against the noonday glare, but you know it is there -- the way you know the conspirators are there, watching, waiting, planning their next move.

You touch the seal ring on your finger. Voice of the Throne. That is what they call you.

It is time to speak.

You turn from the window and walk to the door. Your hand closes on the handle. You are ready.

The door opens.

And everything changes.

Spymaster Zara is standing in the corridor. She is not alone. Two of her agents flank her, armed, their faces grim. And Zara herself -- Zara, who never shows emotion, who speaks in flat facts and dry humor and silences that cut deeper than any blade -- Zara looks frightened.

You have never seen Zara look frightened. In this moment, it is the most terrifying thing you have ever witnessed.

"Vizier," she says. "We need to move. Now."

"What's happened?"

She steps closer. Her voice drops to a whisper, barely audible above the distant splash of fountains.
