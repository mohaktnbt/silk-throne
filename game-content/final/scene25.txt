*comment === OPENING: THE BRIEFING ===

The night is a living thing in Khazara. It breathes through the alleys, exhales fog from the river, and swallows the Citadel's white walls in shadow. You stand in the intelligence chamber -- a windowless room beneath the Tower of Stars that most courtiers do not know exists -- and stare at the map Zara has pinned to the wall.

It is not a map of geography. It is a map of people.

Names. Connections. Red threads linking informant to handler, handler to paymaster, paymaster to the conspiracy's beating heart. Months of work reduced to pins and string, and still the web is incomplete.

"Three cells," Zara says from the corner where she leans against the wall, arms crossed. She has not slept. You can see it in the bruised skin beneath her eyes, in the way she holds herself as though stillness requires effort. "Operating in the Old City, the River District, and the Foreign Quarter. Twelve agents we've identified. Perhaps twenty we haven't."

*page_break

She pushes off the wall and approaches the map, tapping a cluster of pins near the river.

"Tonight they move. My sources say they're consolidating -- burning documents, shifting safe houses, preparing to disappear before the final strike. If we lose them tonight, we lose them permanently."

Secretary Farid hovers near the door, clutching a stack of reports to his chest like armor. "My -- my lord Vizier, the hour is very late. Perhaps we could coordinate from the Citadel? It's -- the Old City at night is not precisely safe."

Zara does not look at him. "Nothing is safe, Farid. That's rather the point."

She turns to you, and in the lamplight her eyes are dark and steady as river stones. "I need a decision. My network is stretched thin. Lila's street children have located two of the three cells. The third -- the one in the Foreign Quarter -- is the most dangerous. That's where their handler operates. A woman called the Weaver."

*page_break

"The Weaver has been feeding intelligence to the Northern Khanates for months. She's the reason General Kadir's garrison rotations keep ending up in enemy hands. She's the reason fifty soldiers died at Fort Darband."

Zara pauses. Chooses her next words with the precision of a woman selecting a blade.

"I can take two cells tonight. Not three. Not with the people I have left. The conspiracy's purge of my network saw to that." A muscle tightens in her jaw -- the only sign of the rage that must burn beneath that controlled exterior. "So I need you to decide. Do you come with me into the Old City and help take the third cell? Or do you coordinate from here, directing my agents by messenger, and accept that the Weaver may escape?"

The question hangs in the incense-thick air. Beyond the chamber walls, you can hear the distant drum of the garrison's night watch, steady as a heartbeat.
