*comment === THE JADE THRONE ROOM, AFTER THE BATTLE ===

The Jade Throne Room is quiet.

You have never heard it quiet before. Every other time you entered this chamber, it hummed with the murmur of courtiers, the rustle of silk, the careful measured breathing of people who understood that a single word spoken out of turn could end a career or begin a war. Now the only sound is your own footsteps on the marble floor.

The battle is over. The siege -- or the trial, or the shadow war, or whatever shape the confrontation took -- has ended. The conspirators are dead, or captured, or fled into the night. The empire still stands, though it stands on shaken foundations.

*page_break

You walk slowly down the center of the Jade Throne Room. The great hall stretches before you, impossibly long, the polished marble floor reflecting the light from the high windows in pale golden ribbons. Dust motes drift through the shafts of sunlight. Somewhere, incense is still burning -- sandalwood and frankincense, the scent of authority. It smells like the first day you arrived.

The geometric tilework beneath your feet -- blue and turquoise and gold -- is cracked in three places. A column near the western wall bears a sword-mark. One of the high windows is shattered, and through the broken pane you can hear the distant sound of the city: voices calling, hammers striking, the first tentative sounds of a capital putting itself back together.

The Jade Throne sits on its dais at the far end of the hall. Carved from a single block of white jade so pale it seems to glow, it waits in silence. Empty. The silk cushion on its seat is askew. A smear of blood mars the lowest step of the dais -- someone fought here, or fell here, in the final hours.

You remember his nervous smile, his stammering briefing about protocol and precedence, the way the setting sun turned the city's domes to copper and gold.

You were a provincial administrator then. An outsider. An empty vessel, as the Dowager said, to be filled with whatever was needed.

You are not empty now.

*comment === THE WEIGHT OF THE MOMENT ===

The Jade Throne Room. The empire's heart.

Three hundred years of history press down on this marble floor. Roshan the Conqueror sat on that jade chair and declared himself emperor. His descendants held it through golden ages and civil wars, through plague and prosperity, through the rise of the printing press and the building of the Grand Bazaar. Every great decision that shaped this empire was made in this room.

Now it waits for you.

The Emperor stands to your left -- a young man whose entire life has been shaped by this chair, this room, this weight. He looks at you, and the question in his eyes is the question that has defined your journey from the moment you passed through the gates of Khazara:

*What happens now?*
