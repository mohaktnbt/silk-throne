That night, you stand on the balcony of your chambers and look out over Khazara. The city's lamps are coming alive, one by one, like stars falling to earth. The first move is made. The Emperor has issued his first independent command. General Kadir stands with you, at least for now. The Dowager has been outmaneuvered or accommodated, but either way she has been put on notice: the era of ruling through her son is drawing to a close.

But you are not naive enough to think this was easy. This was one decree. One small assertion of imperial authority. The Dowager has been building her power for thirty years. The conspiracy has been at work for months, perhaps years. And somewhere in the shadows, the Ghost is watching.

Farid appears at your side with a cup of cardamom tea. He does not speak. He simply stands there, steady and reliable as the Citadel's foundations, and hands you the cup.

The long game has begun.

That the river the Dowager is so fond of quoting can, eventually, wear the stone to nothing.
