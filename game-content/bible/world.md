# The Silk Throne -- World Bible

## The Khazaran Empire

### Overview

The Khazaran Empire is the wealthiest and most powerful civilization in the known world. Spanning from the Ashghar Mountains in the west to the Jade Sea in the east, from the Northern Steppe to the Southern Desert, it commands the only land route between the great trading civilizations of East and West. Every bolt of silk, every sack of spice, every chest of gems that moves between continents passes through Khazaran hands -- and Khazaran tax collectors.

The empire is three centuries old, founded by conquest, maintained by commerce, and justified by culture. Its poets are quoted in every court. Its astronomers map the heavens with instruments no other nation can build. Its architects raise domes and minarets that travelers call the work of gods. And at the center of it all sits the city of Khazara: two million souls, a hundred markets, a thousand fountains, and one throne carved from a single block of white jade.

The Silk Throne.

## Geography

### The Imperial Heartland

The empire occupies a vast landmass roughly equivalent to the combined territories of Mughal India and the Ottoman Empire. The heartland is a fertile river basin -- the Valley of the Ashar -- watered by two great rivers (the Ashar and the Tavan) that converge near the capital. This valley produces grain, cotton, and fruit in staggering abundance, feeding the empire's enormous population.

**Key geographic features:**

- **The Ashar River** -- The lifeblood of the empire. Wide, slow, navigable for most of its length. Trade barges carry goods from the eastern ports to the capital. Crocodiles and river dolphins inhabit its lower reaches.
- **The Tavan River** -- Faster, narrower, running from the northern highlands. Its waters are colder and cleaner. The imperial water supply comes from the Tavan via a system of aqueducts built two centuries ago.
- **The Valley of the Ashar** -- Broad, flat, intensely cultivated. Rice paddies, cotton fields, orchards of mango, pomegranate, and fig. Villages every few miles. The richest agricultural land in the known world.

### The Capital: Khazara

Population approximately two million. Built where the Ashar and Tavan rivers meet, the city sprawls across both banks and several islands. Connected by seventeen bridges, the oldest of which (the Bridge of Founders) is wide enough for six carriages abreast and lined with shops.

**Districts:**

- **The Citadel (Arg-e-Shahi)** -- The imperial palace complex. A city within a city. White marble walls forty feet high. Inside: the Jade Throne Room, the Hall of Mirrors, the Garden of Eternal Spring, the Tower of Stars (observatory), the Dowager's Wing, the Council Chamber, and hundreds of rooms for courtiers, servants, and guards. The player lives and works here.
- **The Grand Bazaar** -- The largest covered market in the world. A labyrinth of corridors, courtyards, and vaulted halls. Organized by trade: the Silk Quarter, the Spice Market, the Gem Sellers' Row, the Book Alley. Guildmaster Priya's domain.
- **The Temple Quarter** -- Seven great temples arranged in a circle around the Eternal Flame, which has burned without interruption for 300 years. High Priest Ashoka presides from the Temple of Seven Wisdoms.
- **The Scholars' Quarter** -- The Great Library, the university, printing houses, astronomical observatories, debate halls. Where Asha spends most of her time.
- **The Foreign Quarter** -- Embassies, trading houses, inns. Ambassador Chen and other foreign dignitaries reside here. A cosmopolitan neighborhood where a dozen languages are spoken.
- **The Old City** -- The original settlement before the empire. Narrow winding streets, ancient buildings, hidden courtyards. The city's poor live here, along with its criminals. Where Spymaster Zara's network operates.
- **The Garrison** -- Military barracks, training grounds, armories. On the northern bank. General Kadir's headquarters.
- **The River District** -- Docks, warehouses, shipyards. Where goods arrive from the eastern ports.

### The Western Mountains (Ashghar Range)

A massive mountain chain forming the empire's western border. Passes through these mountains are the choke points for all East-West trade. The empire controls every pass with fortified garrisons. Beyond the mountains lies the Western Confederation.

Key locations:
- **Ashghar Pass** -- The widest and most-used trade route. A small city has grown up around the customs house.
- **Eagle's Nest** -- A fortress perched on a mountain peak, controlling the northern pass. Nearly impregnable.
- **The Mines of Kuhsar** -- Gem mines in the mountains. Rubies, sapphires, emeralds. A significant source of imperial wealth.

### The Eastern Coast

The empire's eastern edge touches the Jade Sea, a warm body of water connecting to ocean trade routes. Port cities handle incoming goods from distant eastern civilizations.

Key locations:
- **Port Tavandar** -- The largest eastern port. Silk, porcelain, tea, and lacquerware arrive here from across the Jade Sea.
- **The Shipyards of Darya** -- The imperial navy's home base. Modest compared to the army -- the empire is primarily a land power.

### The Northern Steppe

Vast grasslands stretching north of the empire. Sparsely populated, bitterly cold in winter. Home to the Northern Khanates -- nomadic horse warriors who periodically raid the empire's northern provinces. General Kadir considers them the primary military threat.

Key locations:
- **The Wall of Roshan I** -- A centuries-old defensive wall along the northern border. Crumbling in places. Named after the current emperor's ancestor.
- **Fort Darband** -- The primary garrison on the northern frontier. Where Navid was stationed before being recalled to the capital.

### The Southern Desert

Arid wasteland stretching to the southern horizon. Beyond it lie the Southern Kingdoms -- old enemies of the empire, separated by geography that makes large-scale warfare difficult.

Key locations:
- **The Oasis of Zahra** -- A trading post at the edge of the desert. Caravans rest here before the crossing.
- **The Ruins of Old Parvana** -- Remnants of a civilization that predated the empire. Scholars debate whether it was the source of the Eternal Flame religion.

## History

### The Founding (approximately 300 years before game start)

The empire was founded by Roshan the Conqueror (Roshan I), a tribal warlord from the western mountains who united the valley kingdoms through a combination of military genius, strategic marriages, and ruthless efficiency. He established Khazara as his capital, built the first citadel, and proclaimed himself Emperor of All the Land Between the Mountains and the Sea.

**Key historical periods:**

### The Age of Conquest (Years 1-80)

Roshan I and his immediate successors expanded the empire to roughly its current borders. The Northern Steppe raids were beaten back, the eastern coast was absorbed, and the mountain passes were fortified. This era is remembered as glorious but brutal. The empire's military traditions date from this period.

### The Golden Age (Years 80-200)

A long period of peace and prosperity. The Silk Road trade routes were formalized. The Grand Bazaar was built. The Temple of Seven Wisdoms was constructed. The Great Library was established. Poetry, astronomy, and philosophy flourished. The printing press was invented during this period (approximately 50 years before the game starts), triggering a cultural revolution still in progress.

### The Age of Intrigue (Years 200-295)

As the empire matured, external threats diminished but internal politics became increasingly complex. Three civil wars were fought over succession. The Council of Five was established to balance power. The Dowager Empress tradition began -- powerful mothers ruling through young sons. The intelligence network (now commanded by Zara) was formalized.

### The Present Day (Year ~300)

Emperor Roshan IV (the player's emperor) has just taken the throne at age sixteen following the sudden death of his father, Emperor Darius III. The death was officially attributed to fever, but rumors of poison circulate. The player arrives as the newly appointed Grand Vizier -- the empire's chief administrator, second in authority only to the Emperor himself.

The empire faces a convergence of threats:
- The Northern Khanates are raiding with unprecedented frequency and coordination
- The Western Confederation is attempting to break the empire's trade monopoly
- The Southern Kingdoms have a new, ambitious king
- Internally, factions jockey for control of the young emperor
- The predecessor Vizier died under mysterious circumstances

## Religion: The Eternal Flame

### Overview

The dominant religion of the Khazaran Empire is the Faith of the Eternal Flame, a polytheistic system with strong philosophical underpinnings. It is not dogmatic in the way of Western religions -- it prizes wisdom, debate, and personal enlightenment alongside ritual worship.

### Core Beliefs

- **The Eternal Flame** -- A sacred fire that has burned in the Temple of Seven Wisdoms for 300 years (since the empire's founding). It represents the divine spark of creation, truth, and justice. Letting it go out would be the worst omen imaginable.
- **The Seven Wisdoms** -- Seven virtues that the faithful aspire to: Justice, Mercy, Courage, Temperance, Knowledge, Beauty, and Harmony. Each has its own temple within the Temple Quarter, arranged in a circle around the Eternal Flame.
- **The Wheel of Fate** -- The concept that all souls are reborn in an endless cycle. Good deeds elevate the soul; evil deeds degrade it. The ultimate goal is to step off the Wheel entirely and merge with the divine fire.
- **The Celestial Court** -- A pantheon of lesser gods and spirits who embody aspects of the natural world. The sun, moon, rivers, mountains, and seasons all have patron spirits. Common people pray to these spirits for practical needs; the educated tend toward a more philosophical interpretation.

### Religious Structure

- **High Priest/Priestess** -- Currently Ashoka. Presides over the Temple of Seven Wisdoms and the Eternal Flame. Commands the temple guards (a significant armed force). Hears confessions of the powerful.
- **The Seven Masters** -- One for each Wisdom. They oversee their individual temples and teach their particular virtue.
- **Temple Scholars** -- Priests who focus on study and debate rather than ritual. Asha is one of these.
- **Temple Guards** -- An armed religious order. Well-trained, fiercely loyal to the High Priest. Roughly 2,000 in the capital.
- **Mendicant Monks** -- Wandering holy people who have renounced possessions. Respected by the common folk. Sometimes used as messengers or spies.

### Religious Practices

- **The Morning Flame** -- A daily prayer at sunrise, facing the Temple of Seven Wisdoms.
- **Confession** -- Powerful figures confess to the High Priest in a ceremony called "Unburdening." This gives the temple enormous political leverage through accumulated secrets.
- **The Festival of Lights** -- The empire's greatest holiday, held at the autumn equinox. Thousands of oil lamps are lit across the city. The Grand Festival in Scene 15 occurs during this event.
- **Funeral Rites** -- The dead are cremated, their ashes scattered in the river. The soul is believed to ride the smoke upward to the Celestial Court.

## Technology

### Military Technology

- **Gunpowder** -- Exists and is used in cannons and primitive firearms. However, the technology is unreliable, expensive, and slow to reload. Swords, bows, and cavalry remain the dominant military forces. Gunpowder is significant for siege warfare.
- **Steel** -- High-quality steel is produced in the empire. Khazaran swords and armor are prized worldwide.
- **Fortifications** -- The empire's walls and fortresses are engineered to withstand cannon fire, with sloped walls and star-shaped bastions.

### Civilian Technology

- **The Printing Press** -- Invented approximately 50 years ago. Books are now produced in large quantities. Literacy is spreading beyond the elite. This is transforming politics, religion, and culture. Pamphlets and broadsheets are a new and destabilizing force.
- **Astronomical Observatories** -- The Tower of Stars in the Citadel is the finest observatory in the world. The empire's astronomers can predict eclipses, track planetary movements, and navigate by the stars with remarkable precision.
- **Water Engineering** -- Aqueducts, fountains, underground channels (qanat-style). The capital's water system is an engineering marvel. Running water in the Citadel.
- **Paper and Ink** -- High-quality paper production. The empire's calligraphy is considered an art form.
- **Glass** -- Clear glass for windows, mirrors, lenses. The Hall of Mirrors in the Citadel uses glass imported from the Western Confederation, but the empire is developing its own production.
- **Mechanical Clocks** -- Rare and expensive. A few exist in the Citadel. Most people tell time by the sun and the call to prayer.
- **Navigation** -- Compass, astrolabe, detailed charts. The imperial navy, though small, is technologically capable.

## Culture

### Language

The empire has a dominant official language (Khazari) used in court, law, and commerce. However, the empire encompasses many peoples and dozens of regional languages. Educated people typically speak at least three languages.

Key languages:
- **Khazari** -- The court language. Elaborate, formal, with distinct registers for speaking to superiors, equals, and inferiors.
- **Trade Tongue** -- A simplified pidgin used across the Silk Road. Everyone in commerce speaks it.
- **Classical Parvani** -- The ancient language of the pre-imperial civilization. Used in religious texts and scholarship. Equivalent to Latin in medieval Europe.
- **Western Common** -- The language of the Western Confederation. Ambassador Chen speaks it fluently.

### Poetry and Literature

Poetry is the supreme art form in the Khazaran Empire. Generals quote poetry in battle. Merchants seal deals with verses. Court politics is conducted through elaborate poetic exchanges where metaphor carries meaning too dangerous to speak plainly.

Key forms:
- **The Ghazal** -- A love poem with a specific rhyme structure. Leila's specialty.
- **The Qasida** -- A longer poem of praise or lament. Used for political commentary.
- **The Masnavi** -- A narrative poem, often epic in length. The empire's great stories are told in this form.
- **Prose Tales** -- The printing press is creating a new market for prose fiction, which traditionalists consider inferior to poetry.

### Art and Architecture

- **Miniature Painting** -- Detailed, colorful illustrations. Used in books, on walls, on tiles.
- **Calligraphy** -- Writing as art. The finest calligraphers are national treasures.
- **Architecture** -- Domes, minarets, arched colonnades, geometric tilework, garden design. The Citadel is the supreme example.
- **Music** -- Complex modal system. Court musicians perform at all state occasions. The lute (tar), drum (daf), and flute (ney) are principal instruments.
- **Garden Design** -- Formal gardens with fountains, channels, fruit trees, and flowers are a mark of wealth and taste. The Garden of Eternal Spring in the Citadel is said to be the most beautiful garden in the world.

### Court Etiquette

Court etiquette in the Khazaran Empire is Byzantine in complexity. A misplaced word, an improper bow, or a failure to observe precedence can end a career. The player must navigate this system as Grand Vizier.

Key rules:
- **Forms of Address** -- The Emperor is "Light of the World" or "Shadow of God upon the Earth." The Dowager is "Mother of the Empire." The Vizier (the player) is "Voice of the Throne." Each council member has specific titles.
- **Precedence** -- Seating, speaking order, and physical proximity to the Emperor are strictly regulated and politically significant.
- **Gift-Giving** -- Gifts are political statements. Their value, nature, and timing carry meaning. Refusing a gift is a grave insult.
- **The Bow** -- Different depths of bow for different ranks. The Emperor bows to no one. The Vizier bows only to the Emperor and the Dowager.
- **Clothing** -- Colors, fabrics, and jewelry indicate rank. Only the Emperor may wear pure white. Only the Vizier may carry a seal ring.
- **Dining** -- Seating at the imperial table is a map of political power. Being moved closer to the Emperor is a promotion; being moved away is a warning.

### The Council of Five

The Council of Five are the empire's most powerful officials, each commanding a pillar of state power. They answer to the Vizier in theory and to the Emperor in practice, but each has their own power base that makes them semi-independent.

- **Commander of the Armies (General Kadir)** -- Controls the military: 100,000 regular troops, plus provincial militias. The largest armed force but bound by tradition to obey civilian authority.
- **Master of the Treasury / Guildmaster (Priya)** -- Controls taxation, trade regulation, the mint, and the imperial treasury. Technically appointed by the Emperor, but the trade guilds' support is essential.
- **High Priest of the Eternal Flame (Ashoka)** -- Controls the temples, the temple guards (~2,000), the confession system, religious festivals, and the moral authority of the faith.
- **Director of the Eyes (Spymaster Zara)** -- Controls the intelligence network: spies, informants, code-breakers, assassins (officially denied). The smallest apparatus but arguably the most dangerous.
- **Lord Speaker of the Nobility (VACANT)** -- Represents the noble houses in council. This seat is vacant at game start because the previous holder died alongside the old emperor. Multiple nobles compete for the position, and appointing one is an early political challenge for the player.

## Economy

### Sources of Wealth

The empire's wealth comes from three sources, in order of importance:

1. **Trade Tariffs** -- The empire controls the only viable land route between East and West. Every merchant caravan pays tariffs at the mountain passes, at provincial borders, and at city gates. This alone funds the military.

2. **Domestic Production** -- The empire produces silk, cotton, spices (especially pepper, cinnamon, and cardamom), gems (rubies, sapphires, emeralds from the mountain mines), steel, paper, books, and high-quality textiles.

3. **Agricultural Tax** -- The Valley of the Ashar is enormously productive. Peasant farmers pay a tax in grain, which feeds the cities and the army.

### Trade Goods

| Good | Source | Destination | Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Silk | Eastern imports + domestic | Western Confederation | Extremely high |
| Spices | Southern provinces, imports | Everywhere | Very high |
| Gems | Mountain mines | Everywhere | Extremely high |
| Gunpowder | Domestic production | Military only (export banned) | Strategic |
| Books | Domestic (printing press) | Everywhere | Growing |
| Steel | Domestic | Western Confederation, Khanates | High |
| Cotton/Textiles | Domestic | Everywhere | Moderate |
| Horses | Northern imports | Military, domestic | High |

### Currency

- **The Gold Ashraf** -- Standard gold coin, bearing the Emperor's face.
- **The Silver Dirham** -- Common silver coin. Ten dirhams to one ashraf.
- **The Copper Fils** -- Small change. Twenty fils to one dirham.

### Economic Tensions

- The Western Confederation is attempting to find a sea route around the empire to bypass trade tariffs. If they succeed, the empire's primary revenue source collapses.
- The printing press is disrupting traditional scribe guilds and creating new economic power centers.
- The noble houses resent merchant wealth. Priya represents a rising class that the old aristocracy views with contempt.
- Slavery is economically significant in agriculture and mining, but a growing reform movement (connected to the Revolutionary route) seeks abolition.

## Neighboring Powers

### The Western Confederation

A loose alliance of city-states and small kingdoms west of the Ashghar Mountains. Culturally distinct from the empire -- they value individualism, republican government, and maritime commerce. Their technology is roughly equivalent to the empire's, and in some areas (glass-making, clockwork, naval engineering) they are superior.

**Relationship to the empire:** Trade rivals. They resent paying tariffs and are actively seeking alternative trade routes. They are not militarily capable of challenging the empire on land, but their navy is formidable. Ambassador Chen represents a diplomatic relationship that is polite on the surface and hostile underneath.

**Military threat level:** Low (direct), High (economic)

### The Northern Khanates

A collection of nomadic horse-warrior tribes occupying the vast steppe north of the empire. Individually weak, but when united under a strong Khan, they become a formidable raiding force. They fight from horseback with compound bows and curved swords.

**Relationship to the empire:** Hostile. They raid the northern provinces for plunder, slaves, and horses. The Wall of Roshan I was built to contain them but has fallen into disrepair. General Kadir has spent his career fighting them and considers them the primary military threat.

**Military threat level:** High (raiding), Moderate (invasion -- they lack siege capability)

**Secret:** During the game, it is revealed that someone within the empire has been supplying the Khanates with information about border garrison rotations, enabling their raids. This is part of the central conspiracy.

### The Southern Kingdoms

A collection of small but ancient kingdoms in the arid lands south of the empire. They were the empire's original enemies -- Roshan I conquered some of their northern territories during the founding. They have never forgiven this.

**Relationship to the empire:** Old enmity, currently cold peace. A new king, Vikram the Young, has united several kingdoms and is building an army. The desert crossing makes large-scale invasion difficult from either direction, but not impossible.

**Military threat level:** Moderate (growing)

### Distant Powers

- **The Eastern Empires** -- Far across the Jade Sea. Known mainly through trade goods: silk, porcelain, tea. Cultural exchange is limited but growing.
- **The Island Kingdoms** -- Maritime powers in the southern sea. Pirates and traders in equal measure. Their ships occasionally appear in Khazaran ports.

## The Conspiracy (MAJOR SPOILER)

The central mystery of the game concerns the death of the previous Grand Vizier and the broader conspiracy threatening the empire.

**The truth:** A faction within the empire is working to destabilize the young emperor's rule. They murdered the previous Vizier when he got too close to discovering their network. They are simultaneously:
- Feeding information to the Northern Khanates to intensify raids
- Funding the Western Confederation's search for alternative trade routes
- Positioning a rival claimant (Prince Kaveh) to seize the throne

**The mastermind:** The conspiracy's leader depends on the player's route and is revealed in Act 3, Scene 26. The identity shifts based on narrative logic:
- Loyalist route: The Dowager Empress Nur is revealed as the mastermind, having decided the young emperor is too weak and the empire needs a stronger hand (hers, through Kaveh as puppet).
- Kingmaker route: Lord Reza, the traditionalist noble, leads a counter-conspiracy to prevent the player from seizing power.
- Revolutionary route: A coalition of threatened interests -- noble houses, conservative temple elders, and corrupt military officers -- unite against the player's reforms.

This structure ensures the antagonist is personally meaningful to each route.

## Atmosphere and Sensory Details

Writers should evoke the following sensory palette throughout the game:

### Sights
- White marble, gold leaf, geometric tilework in blue and turquoise
- Silk banners in imperial crimson
- Fountains catching sunlight, gardens in precise geometric patterns
- Candlelight and oil lamps in the evening, casting long shadows
- The Eternal Flame visible from every part of the Temple Quarter
- Crowded markets, narrow alleys, wide ceremonial avenues

### Sounds
- Fountains splashing, birds singing in garden courtyards
- The call to prayer echoing across the city at sunrise
- The murmur of the bazaar -- haggling, laughter, the clink of coins
- Court music: the liquid notes of the tar, the steady pulse of the daf
- Distant drums from the garrison during morning drill

### Smells
- Incense (sandalwood, frankincense) in temples and palace
- Spices in the bazaar -- cinnamon, cardamom, saffron, pepper
- Jasmine and rose from the gardens
- Fresh bread and roasting meat from the kitchens
- Old paper and ink in the library
- Horse and leather from the garrison

### Tastes
- Spiced wine (the court drink)
- Sweet tea with cardamom
- Pomegranate, fig, and date
- Saffron rice with lamb
- Rosewater sherbet
- Strong coffee (a new import, fashionable)

### Textures
- Cool marble underfoot
- Heavy silk robes
- The weight of the Vizier's seal ring
- Rough parchment of old documents
- The smoothness of jade (the throne)
- Cold steel of a hidden dagger

## Key Locations for Gameplay

These locations appear repeatedly in scenes and should be described consistently:

1. **The Jade Throne Room** -- Vast, domed chamber. The throne sits on a raised dais. Sunlight enters through high windows. The floor is polished marble with geometric patterns in gold. Courtiers stand in ranked rows. The air smells of incense.

2. **The Council Chamber** -- A smaller, more intimate room. A round table (symbolizing equality among council members, though seating is still political). Maps on the walls. A large window overlooking the Garden of Eternal Spring.

3. **The Garden of Eternal Spring** -- The Citadel's greatest garden. Channels of water in geometric patterns. Fruit trees, roses, jasmine. Peacocks. Marble benches. A popular location for private conversations because the sound of water masks whispered words.

4. **The Grand Bazaar** -- Overwhelming sensory assault. Narrow corridors, high vaulted ceilings, shafts of light from openings above. The smell of spices, the sound of haggling, the glitter of gold jewelry and gems.

5. **The Tower of Stars** -- The observatory. A spiral staircase leads to a domed chamber open to the sky. Astronomical instruments of brass and bronze. Scholar Miriam works here. Quiet, remote, a good place for secret meetings.

6. **The Library** -- Thousands of books and scrolls. The smell of old paper. Quiet, studious atmosphere. Hidden alcoves where one can read -- or eavesdrop -- unseen.

7. **The Old City** -- Narrow streets, overhanging buildings that nearly touch above, shadows even at midday. The smell of cooking and sewage. Street children, beggars, pickpockets. Where Zara's informant network is thickest.

8. **The Dowager's Wing** -- Lavish apartments in the eastern part of the Citadel. Decorated in the Dowager's personal taste: dark wood, gold, deep red silk. Incense is always burning. Visitors feel watched.

9. **The Garrison Yard** -- Open ground for drilling. The sound of clashing steel, shouted commands. Dust in the air. Weapons racks, archery targets, a combat ring. Where Navid trains.

10. **The Temple of Seven Wisdoms** -- Seven interconnected chambers around a central courtyard where the Eternal Flame burns in a great bronze brazier. Each chamber represents one Wisdom. The air is thick with incense. Voices echo. The temple guards stand at every entrance.
